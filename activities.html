FAQ:

Do I need to know chess?

No. The game masters will tell you what moves to make. Being able to count, and to distinguish left from right, will be very useful.

Can I sign up for Cosplay Chess and Cosplay Showcase?

Yes. Selection for each is separate, and the events will not clash.

Can I register on the spot?

Unfortunately, we need exactly 32 people. So we cannot allow on-spot registration.

Do I have to dress up as a chess piece?

No, this is not required.

Can I bring a real weapon (sword / spear / knife / holy hand-grenade of Antioch)?

NO. Do not bring weapons or props that can injure people. Foam props are alright Bringing or using real weapons is immediate grounds for disqualification. Also please make sure that your costume does not contain any protruding parts (trailing ribbons, etc.) that could trip or get in the way of other participants.

What if I don't live in Bangalore?

Please apply, we can make special considerations if you are selected, details are in the form. Please mention your requests or questions in the form field or reach out to popculture@japanhabba.org for any doubts and queries. Mention your name, which days you plan to attend, and where you are from.

Will Cosplay Chess be in conflict with other Cosplay Events?

No, all the conflict events are at separate times and are not in conflict with each other.

Format:

Each contestant will have 30 seconds to perform and 1-2 minutes for a Q&A with the judges and audience. Be sure to keep your performance sharp—timing is crucial, and we'll keep things moving to ensure a smooth flow.

Full Description:

Join us for an engaging 30-minute exploration of the fascinating connections between two of Asia's most influential film industries—Indian and Japanese cinema. This presentation delves into the shared history, mutual inspirations, and cultural exchanges that have shaped these vibrant cinematic traditions over the decades. We'll uncover:

  • The Shared Historical Journey: How Indian and Japanese filmmakers have used cinema to reflect societal transformations, rebuild post-war identities, and preserve cultural heritage.
  • Cultural Crossovers and Inspirations: From the Indian Film Industry's influence in Japan, exemplified by Rajinikanth's fandom, to the Japanese storytelling ethos seen in anime and films resonating with Indian audiences.
  • Parallel Storytelling Traditions: The emphasis on family, emotion, and universal themes in classics like Tokyo Story and Kabhi Khushi Kabhie Gham.
  • Mutual Impact on Global Cinema: How Indian directors drew inspiration from Kurosawa's masterpieces, like Seven Samurai inspiring Sholay, and how Japanese filmmakers incorporate vibrant, dramatic styles akin to Indian musicals.
  • The Evolving Collaboration: A glimpse into Indo-Japanese cinematic partnerships like Ramayana: The Legend of Prince Ram, and the growing appreciation for each other's film styles in global festivals.

OpenUtau Workshop

Ever wondered how Hatsune Miku sings? Is Vocaloid too expensive for you to buy? Worry not! This is the workshop for you.

Learn about voice synths, learn to make them sing, and even make your own voice — all for free. Learn to give your melodies a voice that's truly yours! Open to everyone of all skill levels.

Description:

OpenUtau is a free and open source voice synthesiser software that is used to make songs and covers with virtual voices. The software works as a free alternative to paid singing synthesiser software like VOCALOID, CEVIO, Synthesizer V, etc. Through this workshop, attendees will learn how such softwares operate, as well as how unique voices can be made and loaded into OpenUtau.

The workshop will also include a demonstration of Hanakoloid, the first OpenUtau compatible voice with Indian language capabilities!

Pre-requisites:

  • It is ideal for participants to bring a laptop to this workshop.
  • If you are bringing a laptop, please try and have OpenUtau already installed.
  • You can download OpenUtau from this link: https://www.openutau.com/ (Link contains installation guides as well).
  • Laptops with the software installed are not mandatory for attendance. However it will make following along with the workshop easier.

Please note: This is an unofficial workshop organised by members of India-39, a Vocaloid and voice synthesis fan group. We have no official ties to OpenUtau, Vocaloid, or any other singing synthesiser software. All resources used are free and/or legal to demonstrate. India-39 does not earn any money or profit in any form from this workshop.

Venue:

Event Stage East

The participants will:

  1. Learn to utilize OpenUtau to create original and cover music
  2. Learn to create Utau voice synth banks from scratch
  3. Experience the burgeoning Indian vocaloid community first, by interacting with creators, and fellow fans.

Workshop flow:

  1. Intro to vocal synth
    1. Miku, and other vocaloids
    2. limitations(price)
  2. OpenUtau
    1. Introduction
    2. Installation
    3. Explaining tools, interface , USTX stuff
    4. Notes, lyrics
    5. Parameters, exporting
    6. Play the song
    7. Audience session

What's the difference between Western and Japanese animation? What made 90s anime look so good? Why does anime look so different now? Learn the answers to all these questions and more on this talk on the stylistic evolution of animation in anime.

Full Description:

From the early theatrical shorts of the 1920s to the present day, the stylistic evolution of animation can be broadly described as a struggle between two competing philosophies, realism versus abstraction. Realism extends beyond just complex character designs, involving nuanced character acting and even lighting, depth of field and parallax, whereas abstraction in animation subverts reality to try to convey a feeling to audience.

This talk explores the history of anime and animation in general to learn how realism and abstraction evolved through eras of western and japanese animation. By looking at how different directors and animators approached these two conflicting philosophies, their stylistic influences and their intentions in using a specific animation style, the talk aims to impart an understanding and appreciation of animation in anime.

Bonus impact frame quiz at end of talk.

Rooted in centuries of Japanese tradition, bonsai represents the harmonious bond between nature and human artistry — where trees are carefully cultivated to capture the essence of their wild counterparts in a miniature form. Delve into the enchanting world of bonsai—an art form that originated over a thousand years ago in Japan as a way to bring the grandeur of nature into personal spaces. With expert guidance, learn the principles that underpin this ancient art form while gaining practical knowledge about bonsai care and maintenance. Our distinguished practitioners will share insights into selecting the perfect soil and pots, ceramic or otherwise, mastering proper watering techniques, and understanding the essential care practices that keep these living artworks thriving. Toubashi: Build a Tower with Chopsticks

Steady hands and sharp focus! 🥢✨ Is your chopsticks game your chounoryoku?
Race against the clock as you build a tower with wooden blocks using chopsticks in 60 seconds.
Precision is key! Can you handle the pressure? 😅

Rules:

  1. The challenger has to hold the chopstick and wait for the time to start
  2. The volunteer will set the timer for 60 seconds.
  3. In this time frame, the challenger has to pick up wooden blocks with the chopsticks from the tray, place it in a 3x3 orientation and build a tower.
  4. The challenger is not allowed to touch the wooden blocks with their hands at any moment. All blocks are to be picked up, stacked and assembled with chopsticks only
  5. Most number of blocks stacked gets a mention on the leaderboard and a shoutout from Japan Habba

Tips to ace the game

  1. Work on holding chopsticks properly
  2. Keep your other hand in the back to prevent yourself from touching the blocks
  3. If your tower falls down while the clock is running, do not panic. You can always reassemble till the timer goes off :)
